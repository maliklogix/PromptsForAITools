# User Prompts for Mubert

## Prompt 1: Fitness App Workout Music
"Generate a 45-minute workout music stream for a HIIT fitness app. The music should adapt to the workout phases: (1) Warm-up (5 min): medium-energy electronic with a steady beat at 110 BPM, (2) High-intensity intervals (20 min): aggressive, driving EDM/drum & bass alternating between peaks (150 BPM, maximum energy) and 30-second recovery dips (120 BPM, reduced intensity), (3) Strength training (15 min): heavy trap/bass music at 130 BPM with a consistent powerful groove, (4) Cool-down (5 min): smooth ambient chillout gradually decreasing from 100 BPM to 70 BPM. The transitions between phases should be smooth and musical. Energy changes should feel natural, not abrupt."

## Prompt 2: Focus and Productivity Stream
"Create an infinite productivity music stream for a focus app. Genre: ambient electronic with lo-fi textures. The music should be completely unobtrusive — no strong melodies, no sudden changes, no lyrics. Soft synthesizer pads, subtle granular textures, very gentle rhythmic pulses (not full beats), and occasional harmonic shifts every 2-3 minutes. Tempo: 60-70 BPM. Energy level: 2 out of 10. The music should fade into the background of consciousness — the user should forget it's playing, and only notice when it stops. It should adapt to time of day: slightly brighter tones for morning sessions, warmer and darker tones for evening sessions."

## Prompt 3: Mobile Game Adaptive Soundtrack
"Design a generative soundtrack for a space exploration mobile game. I need 4 music layers that blend seamlessly: (1) Menu/Hub: atmospheric space ambient — deep drones, shimmering pads, slow arpeggios, mysterious and vast feeling, energy 3/10, (2) Exploration: medium-energy electronic with a gentle pulse, curious and wonder-filled mood, energy 5/10, add subtle melodic elements, (3) Combat: intense, driving sci-fi action music, pulsing bass, aggressive synths, rapid percussion, energy 8/10, (4) Boss Fight: epic and overwhelming, dystopian atmosphere, distorted bass drops, chaotic percussion, energy 10/10. The transition between any two layers should take 3-5 seconds and feel musically natural. All layers should share the same key (D minor) and be tempo-compatible."

## Prompt 4: Meditation and Sleep App
"Generate a 30-minute guided meditation music stream. Structure: (1) Opening (3 min): gentle bells or singing bowls with light ambient pads to signal the start of the session, energy gradually decreasing, (2) Body scan phase (10 min): deep, sustained drone in D with almost imperceptible harmonic movement, very slow (40 BPM feel), occasional soft nature sounds (distant rain, wind), (3) Deep relaxation (12 min): minimal — barely-there tonal textures, lower frequencies predominating, approaching silence in places, binaural beats at 4 Hz (theta waves), (4) Awakening (5 min): very gradually reintroduce brightness, subtle melodic elements, gently rising energy to bring the listener back. The entire piece should have no sudden changes — everything morphs slowly and gently."

## Prompt 5: Restaurant and Retail Ambience
"Create a 4-hour non-repeating background music stream for an upscale restaurant. Genre: modern jazz/lounge. Smooth jazz piano, soft upright bass, brushed drums, occasional saxophone and trumpet in a muted, intimate style. Tempo: 90-110 BPM with natural variation. Energy: low-medium (3-4 out of 10). The music should enhance conversation, not compete with it. No dramatic dynamics, no fast solos, no sudden changes. The harmonic palette should be rich but undemanding — extended chords (9ths, 13ths), modal interchange. The stream should subtly evolve over 4 hours, keeping the same mood but never literally repeating any musical phrase. It should feel like a world-class live jazz trio playing in the corner."

## Prompt 6: Twitch Live Stream Background
"Generate DMCA-safe background music for a 6-hour Twitch gaming stream. The music should automatically adapt to what's happening: (1) Chill chat segments: lo-fi hip-hop beats, relaxed and cozy, energy 3/10, (2) Casual gameplay: electronic indie with a nice groove, upbeat but not distracting, energy 5/10, (3) Competitive/intense moments: energetic electronic with driving beats and bass, energy 7/10, (4) Hype moments (victories, big plays): celebratory, high-energy drops, energy 9/10. I should be able to switch between modes with a simple command (!chill, !gaming, !intense, !hype). Transitions between modes should take 5-10 seconds and feel smooth. The music should NEVER trigger Twitch DMCA takedowns."
